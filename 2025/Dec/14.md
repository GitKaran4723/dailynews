# Does the Netflix-Warner deal threaten cinema?
## **Netflix‚ÄìWarner Bros Merger: Streaming, Power & the Future of Cinema**

### 1. **Basic Understanding**

**What is the deal?**
On December 5, **Netflix** announced it will acquire **Warner Bros.**, including:

* Film and television studios
* Premium assets like **HBO**

**Deal value**: ~**$82.7 billion**

**Why is this historic?**

* Traditionally:

  * **Old model** ‚Üí Studios produced ‚Üí theatres/TV distributed
* Now:

  * A **streaming-first platform** absorbs a **legacy studio**
* This marks a shift to **full vertical integration**:

  * Creation + ownership + distribution + exhibition under one roof

* **Content creation** ‚Üí in-house studios & talent
* **Ownership** ‚Üí vast Warner + HBO library
* **Distribution** ‚Üí global streaming infrastructure
* **Curation** ‚Üí algorithm-driven recommendations

* Economies of scale

### 1. **Basic Understanding**

**Why is this important?**
For the first time globally, a sovereign state has **legally banned children below 16 years** from accessing major social media platforms. This is a **landmark intervention at the intersection of technology, mental health, child rights, and regulation**.

**Core tension**

* **Protection of children** üÜö **Digital rights, privacy & feasibility**
* **State intervention** üÜö **Parental choice**
* **Public health approach** üÜö **Platform self-regulation**

---

### 2. **The New Law: What Exactly Has Australia Done?**

* In **November 2024**, Australia passed the
  **Online Safety Amendment (Social Media Minimum Age) Bill 2024**
* **Effective date**: **December 10, 2025**
* **Key provisions**:

  * Minimum age of **16 years** for accounts on major social media platforms
  * Parents **cannot override** the ban by giving consent
  * Applies to platforms like:

    * Facebook, Instagram, X, TikTok, Snapchat, YouTube, Reddit, Twitch, Kick
  * Platforms must take **‚Äúreasonable steps‚Äù** to block underage users
  * **Penalty for non-compliance**:

    * Up to **$33 million (A$49.5 million)** per violation

**Political leadership**

* Law introduced and defended by PM **Anthony Albanese**

---

### 3. **Government‚Äôs Rationale**

The Australian government views social media as a **public health risk for children**.

**Identified harms**

* Cyberbullying
* Exposure to harmful & sexualised content
* Online grooming & predatory behaviour
* Addiction-like engagement patterns
* Anxiety, depression, loneliness, social comparison

üëâ The state‚Äôs position:
**Children lack the cognitive maturity to safely navigate algorithm-driven platforms.**

### 4. **How Have Social Media Companies Responded?**

#### a) **Meta‚Äôs response**

* Warned teenagers aged **13‚Äì15** to:

  * Download data
  * Delete accounts
* Plans to notify users when they turn 16
* Claims:

  * A **blanket ban** is ineffective
  * Teenagers will be isolated from online communities
  * Protection will be ‚Äúinconsistent‚Äù

#### b) **Regulatory oversight**

* Australia‚Äôs regulator **Australian Communications and Media Authority**

  * ~1.5 lakh Facebook users aged 13‚Äì15
  * ~3.5 lakh Instagram users in same bracket

### 5. **How Is Age Being Verified?**

This is the **most controversial aspect**.

**Current options discussed**

1. Government-issued ID
2. Facial recognition / video selfie
3. Voice recognition
4. Behavioural inference using user interactions
5. Third-party age verification tools (e.g. **Yoti**)

**Problems identified**

* **False rejections**:

  * Facial recognition falsely blocked:

    * 8.5% of 16-year-olds
    * 2.6% of 17-year-olds
* **Privacy & surveillance risks**
* Data security of minors

Meta suggested Apple & Google Play should verify age at app-store level to reduce privacy risks.

### 6. **What Are the Drawbacks & Criticisms?**

* Collection of biometric data of children

* Risk of pushing children to:

  * Unregulated platforms

#### d) **Digital exclusion**

* Loss of access to:
