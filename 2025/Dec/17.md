# The future of governance in post-Maoist India

---

### 1. **Basic Understanding**

**What is the Maoist (Left-Wing Extremist) movement?**

* Originated with the **Naxalbari uprising (1967)**.
* Present form concentrated in **central and eastern India** (Red Corridor).
* Predominantly operates in **Fifth Schedule Areas** with large **Adivasi populations**.

**Dominant explanation so far**

* Growth attributed mainly to:

  * Poverty
  * Underdevelopment
  * Socio-economic deprivation
* Resulted in a **two-pronged approach** by the State:

  * Security operations
  * Development schemes

**What is missing?**

* **Governance failure** as an independent and central causal factor.

---

### 2. **Core Argument of the Article**

> Maoism did not grow only because people were poor, but because **governance systems failed to deliver justice, rights, representation and dignity**, especially in Fifth Schedule Areas.

### 3. **Fifth Schedule: Vision vs Reality**

#### (a) **Constitutional Vision**

* Same **colonial administrative structures** continued

  * Complex
  * Inaccessible to low-literacy tribal populations
* **Planning Commission Expert Committee (2008)**:

  * Resource-rich region reduced to penury
